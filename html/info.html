Information

  • Yosemite National Park is located in the western part of the United States, in the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.
  • Yosemite was first protected by the federal government in 1864, and it became a national park in 1890.
  • Yosemite covers an area of over 1,200 square miles (3,100 square kilometers).
  • The park is known for its stunning natural beauty, including granite cliffs, waterfalls, and giant sequoia trees.
  • The park is home to several famous landmarks, including Half Dome, El Capitan, and Yosemite Falls.
  • Yosemite is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a variety of recreational activities such as hiking, camping, rock climbing, and skiing.
  • The park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including black bears, mountain lions, coyotes, and mule deer.
  • The Ahwahnee Hotel, a National Historic Landmark, is located in Yosemite Valley and is a popular destination for visitors to the park.
  • Yosemite is also home to a number of Native American sites, including rock art and other cultural artifacts.
  • Yosemite has a rich history of conservation, with many famous environmentalists, including John Muir and Ansel Adams, advocating for the preservation of the park.
  • Yosemite is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts millions of visitors each year from around the world.
